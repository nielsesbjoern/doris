Personal and Business Coaching

In coaching, the initial focus is on clarifying complex situations. Starting from a situational analysis, levels of objectives are defined, courses of action are explored, and the appropriate direction for a solution is developed.

Personal Coaching

  • Personal development
  • Reflection
  • Conflict resolution
  • Professional role performance

Business Coaching

  • Focus
  • Optimisation
  • Strategy

Personal Coaching — Formats

Leaders rarely have the opportunity to attend regular appointments. I address this with intensive settings and complementary measures such as smart coaching or coaching on the job.

Situational Coaching

Support in clarifying complex professional and personal situations — including preparation for important conversations and negotiations.

Onboarding Coaching

Preparation for taking on a new company or department — systematic exploration, avoiding unnecessary friction.

Positioning Coaching

A structural overview of your situation within the organisation and the current requirements.

Task-Focused Coaching

Practical tools for the specific task — with training elements on request.

Personality Coaching

Fostering self-development that often takes a back seat to the leadership role. Reflection on your own person and external impact — supported by potential analyses to focus new objectives.

Therapeutic Coaching

My licence as a therapist enables support even where a coach would otherwise need to refer on — for motivation difficulties, suspected burnout, loss of energy, or inner conflicts. With diagnostic root-cause analysis on request.

Business Coaching and Consulting

Business development takes centre stage. For a change of direction, even small interventions can be significant — integrated with consulting methods for strategic leadership.

Common questions

  • How do I, as an entrepreneur, leader, or HR director, gain an overview of the current situation?
  • Which strategy matters right now — and how do I bring my employees on board?
  • Which measures will achieve the best effect at present?
  • Where do untapped potentials still lie?

Consulting elements

  • Situational analysis
  • Structural contact or conflict analysis
  • Strategy development matrix
  • Action planning
  • Portfolio work
  • Strategic human resources development planning

Process Consulting

I understand consulting primarily as process consulting: drawing out existing knowledge, systematising it, and preparing it conceptually for productive outcomes. Subsequently, I am available as an external project facilitator.

  • Future workshops for developing vision and mission
  • Workshops for strategy development with implementation planning
  • Workshops for capturing impulses for change and optimisation
  • Conducting and evaluating employee surveys with recommendations for action

Psychological Consulting

When psychological questions play a role — for instance in conflict situations or with difficult employee behaviour. Psychology is decisive for the reception and processing of information, especially in organisational communication.
