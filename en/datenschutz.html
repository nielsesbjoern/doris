Data protection on this website

We treat your personal data confidentially and in accordance with statutory data protection regulations and this privacy policy. Use of our website is generally possible without providing personal data. We do not use personal data for marketing or analytics purposes.

Please note that data transmission over the Internet (e.g. when communicating by email) may have security vulnerabilities. Complete protection of data against access by third parties is not possible.

Hosting and server log files

This website is hosted by Vercel Inc., 440 N Barranca Avenue #4133, Covina, CA 91723, USA. When you access our pages, the hosting provider automatically collects information in server log files transmitted by your browser. This may include:

  • IP address
  • date and time of the request
  • page or file accessed
  • browser type and version
  • operating system used
  • referrer URL (previously visited page)

Processing is based on Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR (legitimate interest in the technically error-free and secure provision of our website). Log data is stored only for a limited period and then deleted, unless retention is required for security reasons.

We have concluded a data processing agreement with Vercel. Personal data may be transferred to the USA in this context. Vercel relies on the EU Commission's Standard Contractual Clauses and — where applicable — the EU-US Data Privacy Framework. Further information: vercel.com/legal/privacy-policy.

Cookies and local storage

We do not use cookies and do not use analytics or tracking tools. To store your display and language preferences, however, the website uses your browser's local storage (localStorage) with the keys dg-theme (light/dark mode) and dg-lang (language selection). This data remains on your device, is not transmitted to us, and serves solely to make the website easier to use. The legal basis is Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR (legitimate interest in a user-friendly presentation). You can delete local storage at any time via your browser settings.

Contact

If you contact us by email or phone, we process the data you provide (e.g. name, email address, phone number, content of your enquiry) to handle your request. The legal basis is Art. 6(1)(b) GDPR where your enquiry relates to the performance of a contract or pre-contractual measures, otherwise Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR (legitimate interest in responding to your enquiry). Data is deleted once your enquiry has been fully processed and no statutory retention obligations apply.

Fonts

For consistent typography we use the Inter and Cormorant Garamond typefaces. These are hosted on our web server and loaded from there into your browser. No connection is made to external font providers (e.g. Google Fonts).

Information on data processing in the context of a commission

The following information applies in addition to website use to the processing of personal data in the context of a service commission (coaching, consulting, training, diagnostics).

1. Collection, processing, and storage of personal data

Type, purpose, and use

When you commission us, we collect the following information:

  • Salutation, first name, surname
  • a valid email address
  • Address
  • Telephone number (landline and/or mobile)
  • Information necessary for the provision of the service

This data is collected in order to

  • identify you as our client
  • advise you appropriately and carry out the commissioned service
  • correspond with you
  • issue invoices
  • handle any liability claims and assert any claims against you

Data processing is carried out at your request and is required pursuant to Art. 6 (1) sentence 1 lit. b GDPR for the stated purposes for the appropriate handling of the commission and for the mutual fulfilment of obligations under the service contract.

Where special categories of personal data are processed in the context of therapeutic, diagnostic, or other services (e.g. health data pursuant to Art. 9(1) GDPR), this is done on the basis of your explicit consent pursuant to Art. 9(2)(a) GDPR and/or — where applicable — for healthcare or treatment purposes pursuant to Art. 9(2)(h) GDPR in conjunction with § 22(1) no. 1 lit. b BDSG. You are obliged to provide the data required for concluding a contract and providing the service; without this data, a commission generally cannot be carried out.

No automated decision-making, including profiling within the meaning of Art. 22 GDPR, takes place.

Your data will generally only be stored for as long as required by the purpose of the respective data processing. Extended storage may apply in particular where this is still necessary for legal enforcement by us or from our other legitimate interests, or where we are legally obliged to retain your data (e.g. within statutory retention periods for tax purposes, which are generally 10 years).

2. Disclosure of data to third parties

Transmission of your personal data to third parties involved in the specific service commission only takes place with your consent.

3. Rights of data subjects

You have the right:

  • pursuant to Art. 7 (3) GDPR to withdraw your consent given once at any time. This has the consequence that we may no longer continue data processing based on this consent for the future
  • pursuant to Art. 15 GDPR to request information about your personal data processed by us — in particular concerning processing purposes, categories of data, recipients, storage period, rights to correction, deletion and objection, right to lodge a complaint, data origin, and automated decision-making including profiling
  • pursuant to Art. 16 GDPR to request without delay the correction of inaccurate or the completion of your personal data stored by us
  • pursuant to Art. 17 GDPR to request deletion of your personal data stored by us, insofar as processing is not required for exercising the right to freedom of expression and information, for fulfilling a legal obligation, for reasons of public interest, or for the assertion, exercise, or defence of legal claims
  • pursuant to Art. 18 GDPR to request restriction of processing of your personal data, insofar as you contest the accuracy of the data, processing is unlawful, but you refuse deletion and we no longer need the data, but you require it for the assertion, exercise, or defence of legal claims, or you have lodged an objection to processing pursuant to Art. 21 GDPR
  • pursuant to Art. 20 GDPR to receive your personal data that you have provided to us in a structured, commonly used, and machine-readable format, or to request transmission to another controller
  • pursuant to Art. 77 GDPR to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority

Supervisory authority

If you wish to exercise your right to object, an informal email to dg@doris-gunsch.eu is sufficient.
